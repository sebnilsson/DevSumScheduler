Biography

Sebastian is a full stack developer with a focus on the web, centered around Microsoft-technologies. Starting his professional career in 2003, he identified a lot of inefficient processes and repetitive work, even within the tech industry, so he started learning .NET 1.1 in his spare time. Since 2007 he’s been working professionally as a web developer with a passion for web-standards, HTML5, JavaScript and open-source technologies. He’s got a lot of ongoing personal projects, mostly created to learn new things.

Throughout his career, Sebastian has always promoted the importance of creating the optimal environment for developers, constantly improving the culture of knowledge-sharing, working processes, creativity, and the latest technologies. Giving back to the community has been part of this drive, for example by organizing and hosting Sweden’s largest .NET-meetup to date, with 180 attendees listening to what was upcoming in .NET Core 1.0, around its launch.

Session: Vue: The framework you maybe already knew

When picking what JavaScript-framework to use in your project, you might want to take more parameters into account than just how many users they have today. What about level of entry? New colleagues should be able to pick up the framework relatively fast. What about work-flows? If you need to collaborate outside your team, the framework should support it. Have you switched framework before? Maybe your next choice should take that into account as well.

In this session, you will get some thoughts on different factors to take into account when choosing your future framework, some of which might not be too obvious at first. You’ll get some basics of Vue, many of which should be applicable for these points. All this, of course, will be manifested with the help of a proof-of-concept ToDo-application written in Vue.
